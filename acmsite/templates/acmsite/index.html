The Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) is a global scientific and educational organization dedicated to advancing the art, science, engineering, and application of computing, serving both professional and public interests by fostering the open exchange of information and by promoting the highest professional and ethical standards.

ABOUT

ACM’s broad‐based infrastructure supports more than 860 professional and student chapters worldwide. These "local neighborhoods" offer opportunities for members to gain access to critical research and establish personal networking systems.

CLUBS

At Auburn University’s chapter of ACM, students can participate in clubs that pertain to various computer science topics. Our three clubs, Competitive Programming Team (CPT), Ethical Hacking Club (EHC), and Makerspace, provide valuable experience with programming that students otherwise would not receive through their coursework alone.

EVENTS

Auburn ACM also hosts events, including its annual Auburn Hackathon and monthly Tech Talks. These events allow students to explore computer science further and give insight to the opportunities that are available to students during college and beyond.
